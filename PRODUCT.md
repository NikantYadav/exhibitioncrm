# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Mixed: (1) sales/BD reps working an expo floor — on their feet, often one-handed, capturing leads fast between conversations in a noisy, bright environment; (2) founders/execs networking at conferences and dinners who want quick capture plus smart, low-effort follow-up without looking distracted from the conversation. The app has to serve both fast on-the-floor capture and considered post-event follow-through in the same UI.

## Product Purpose

Exono is a personal assistant + relationship-memory CRM for exhibitions and events. It captures leads (business cards, QR codes, voice notes), remembers interactions over time, enriches contacts/companies with AI research, and turns that into smart follow-ups. Success looks like: nothing captured at an event gets lost, and follow-up actually happens because the app made the next step obvious and low-friction.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, fast, professional. Reference: Attio — crisp data-forward product feel, restrained color use, confident typography, no decorative noise. The app should feel like a precision tool a professional trusts in the field, not a consumer social app and not an enterprise CRM back office.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboard cliches: no gradient-text hero metrics, no cookie-cutter icon+heading card grids, no eyebrow labels stacked above every section.
- Consumer social-app glossiness: no Instagram/TikTok-style glassmorphism, heavy gradients, or playful bounce animations.
- Salesy/CRM-bloat feel (Salesforce-style): avoid dense enterprise back-office clutter, over-nested cards, and excess chrome. This is a fast field tool, not an admin console.

## Design Principles

- **Speed under pressure**: every capture flow assumes the user is mid-conversation or walking the floor — minimize taps, avoid modal stacking, surface the next action immediately.
- **One tool, two tempos**: the same screens must work for rapid-fire capture and for calmer, considered review/follow-up later — don't force one tempo's interaction pattern onto the other.
- **Precision over decoration**: restrained color (the existing blue accent system), confident type hierarchy, no ornamental gradients/glass — Attio-grade data-forward polish, not consumer gloss.
- **Memory should feel effortless**: surfaces that recall past interactions (contact/company detail, follow-ups) should make relationship history legible at a glance, not buried in tabs.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: solid contrast ratios (especially given outdoor/bright-venue use), reasonable tap targets for one-handed/on-the-move use, no unusual accommodation requirements beyond good practice.
